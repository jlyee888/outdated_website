Irvine and Orange County are home to many beautiful and easy-to-access trails offering a quick escape from the suburbs and into nature. Most trails in Irvine fall under the jurisdiction of the Irvine Ranch Conservatory, the OC Parks System, and a few are part of the State Parks System.

The Cleveland National Forest is part of the National Forest system. As such, an Adventure Pass is required to park at any developed parking lot that accesses the park. There are three main regions of Cleveland NF. The Trabuco Ranger District encompasses the Santa Ana Mountains in Riverside and Orange County. It is bounded by the CA 91 freeway in the north and the CA 74 freeway provides access to the Southern half of this region. The Palomar Ranger District is located to the southwest of San Jacinto NF between Pala and Warner Springs, Aguana and Ramona. Lastly, the Descanso Ranger District is located in San Diego County. It houses the first miles of the Pacific Crest Trail.

For more information, check out their Visitor's Guide.

Trabuco Ranger District

The Trabuco Ranger District is the Northernmost wilderness district of the Cleveland National Forest and borders Orange County (Irvine, Rancho Santa Margarita, Corona, etc.) and contains many quickly accessible trails for OC and West Inland-Empire residents. In this section, we will split the Trabuco Ranger District into two parts: North and South.

The North Trabuco Ranger District is everything accessible north of the Ortega CA 74 freeway. The South Trabuco Ranger District is everything accessible from the Ortega CA 74 freeway and south.
